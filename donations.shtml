The Details

  • You must be 70½ or older.
  • You may transfer up to $100,000 from your IRA directly to the University of Illinois Foundation designated for UI Laboratory High School.
  • If you have not yet taken your required minimum distribution for 2016, your gift can satisfy all or part of that requirement.
  • You pay no income tax on the gift. The transfer generates neither taxable income nor a tax deduction, so you benefit even if you do not itemize your tax deductions.
If you are at least 59½ but not yet 70½ you can make a charitable gift from an IRA during your lifetime, without penalty. If you take distributions from your IRA—even if paid directly to the University of Illinois Foundation designated for UI Laboratory High School — you'll have to report the distribution as taxable income on your tax return in the year of distribution. If you itemize your deductions, you can take an income tax charitable deduction for the amount of your gift.

Hover over a fund for a description

    $ 1. Parent Education Fund
    Current use funds provided by parents of students at University Laboratory High School. These funds are unrestricted and are to be used by the Director at his/her discretion.

    $ 2. Annual Fund
    Current use funds provided by alumni, friends and others to be used at the discretion of the Director.

    $ 3. Endowment Fund
    The net income from the Uni High Endowment Fund provides support to special projects designed to enrich the teaching programs and enhance the quality of instruction at the University Laboratory High School, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

    $ 4. John E.Finch, M.D. Faculty Support Fund (endowment)
    Gifts to this endowed fund provide financial support that would not be met by the budget set forth by the school for faculty salaries.

    $ 5. Hedeman Faculty Salary Fund (endowment)
    This fund provides stipends to enhance the salaries of full-time teachers and counselors who have two or more years of service at University Laboratory High School at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

    $ 6. Class of 1984 Leo Millar Memorial Fund
    Class of 1984 Leo Millar Memorial Fund provides funds to improve the present facility. These gifts could be given as current use gifts or can be placed in a named endowment ($25,000 or more).

    $ 7. 4-Square Reunion Fund
    The Worldwide Four-Square Invitational Conference (Uni High alumni 1976 ~ 1986) desires to provide faculty an annual award for innovation in teaching their curriculum. On an annual basis, applications will be made available to the faculty in the Spring Semester, thus allowing funds to be used in the fall of the following year for the new curriculum. Awards are not paid to the grantee (since this would impact taxation) but for supplies/services to effect the innovative teaching.

    $ 8. Innovations in Learning Fund
    Innovations in Learning is a five year fundraising initiative for active learning spaces in the school, and for creating prototype classrooms of the future.

    $ 9. Barbara Lazarus ’63 Memorial Fund
    The Barbara Lazarus ’63 Memorial Fund to encourage, facilitate and support the study by girls at the secondary school level of science, technology, engineering and math (STEM). The goal of those who have initiated the fund, is that the school will use the fund for scholarships, stipends, programs, and research projects or grants that will meet the fund’s goals.

    $ 10. Uni High Class of 1973 Endowment
    The Uni High Class of 1973 is currently an undesignated fund, although the designation of this fund will be determined at the 45th Class Reunion. Classmates are hopeful this fund can generate over $25,000 by 2018 to officially name an endowment in honor of their class.
